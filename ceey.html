Policy for Social Movility

The USim together with the Espinosa Yglesias Research Center have developed a model that analyzes the effects of structural change in the social security system in Mexico on economic growth, inequality and social movility. The model simulates social and economic processes, such as demographic growth, the formation of firms that produce a range of goods, and family formation and dissolution, in order to construct the economic climate of Mexico. Once established this context, we analyzed the effects that different reforms to social security and its financing would have on inequality and social movility.

The model predicts that the transition from the current dual social security system to a universal one would lower the rate of informal employment and, in the medium-long run, increase economic growth and decrease inequality, and slightly increase absolute educational movility.

This model is not currently available to the public.

Project status: Finished.
