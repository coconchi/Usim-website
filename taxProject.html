Tax simulator

Fiscal policy & Inequality

Tax systems are important structures for financing government functioning and services as well as redistributing wealth, altering consumption patterns, and providing (dis)incentives to certain sectors of the economy. However, tax collection is challenging in the developing world, particularly in Mexico, which faces high rates of tax evasion and inequality. These factors distort who comprises the effective tax base both for income and consumption taxes on non-essential goods.

Data-driven analysis

We develop a comprehensive microsimulation model of the Mexican tax system that reproduces official aggregate statistics of tax collection, evasion and inequality from microdata. Our simulator is highly flexible, and permits changes not only to individual rates, but also to subsidies of social programs and exemptions and cuotas for corporate, income and consumption taxes. The tool incorporates a user-friendly interface which permits policy makers and researchers to compare the outcome of different escenarios.

Project status: In final phase.
