Public Policy Network

Integral analysis for policy contexts

Determining the priorities of public policy is challenging because objectives can be interrelated and because of inefficiencies in implementation. The effects of policies are rarely isolated to their respective spheres, and these relationships between policy fields make it difficult to distinguish between efficient and inefficient policies.

In order to inform this complex policy context, the Simulation Unit in partnership with the Alan Turing Institute and UNDP are working to apply the model of Castañeda, Chávez-Juárez y Guerrero (2018) to Mexico at the sub-national level. Ths model posits a network of interdependent policy 'centers' or bureaus which is characterized by spillover effects between policy initiatives. By estimating this network, we are able to identify effecive policy nodes and bottlenecks and thus inform where policy priorities should be placed.
