Traffic in CDMX

Simulation for urban planning solutions

Vehicular flow and traffic are salient issues in urban centers all over the world, and particularly in Mexico City where population and vehicle-ownership growth has resulted in increased raffic. The Simulation Unit is in the process of developing an agent-based model that simulates traffic flow in various areas of Mexico City. The objective of this project is to evaluate ex-ante low-cost policy proposals for easing traffic burdens and reduce the monetary and non-monetary costs associated with it.

In addition to analyzing traditional policy interventions, we also are working on the design of strategies based on behavioral science in partnership with the Innovation, Behavioral and Experimental Unit. These strategies appeal to the untapped capacity of citizens to self- organize in reducing traffic externalities.

Project status: In progress.
